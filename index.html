Scenario 1 • Performance and wellbeing

A strong performer is slipping

You are leading a high-performing team in a busy organisation. Recently, one of your most capable team members has become noticeably withdrawn and has missed two key deadlines. When asked, they say they are “fine”, but they appear defensive and disengaged. At the same time, senior leadership is pushing hard for results and has asked you to lift performance across the board with no extra resources.

Discussion questions

  • What pressure points are shaping this situation?
  • What are the risks of responding too quickly?
  • How do you balance support, fairness and performance?

Workplace application

  • How would you apply this approach in your own workplace if a strong team member suddenly became disengaged?

Scenario 2 • Conflict and team culture

A conflict is affecting team morale

Two respected team members have become increasingly tense with each other during meetings. Their disagreement began over a project decision, but it now shows up in emails, handovers and side conversations. The rest of the team is noticing and some people are starting to avoid speaking up. You need the project to stay on track, but morale is slipping.

Discussion questions

  • What is the impact of leaving this unresolved?
  • How should the leader respond without taking sides too quickly?
  • What does fairness look like in this situation?

Workplace application

  • How would you handle rising tension between two respected colleagues in your own team while keeping trust intact?

Scenario 3 • Pressure and decision-making

A decision must be made quickly

A major client has requested a last-minute change that would put pressure on your team and may affect quality. Senior leadership wants you to agree quickly to protect the relationship. Several team members are already stretched and one has quietly raised concerns that the timeline is unrealistic. You need to respond today.

Discussion questions

  • What are the short-term and long-term risks of saying yes too quickly?
  • How should the leader weigh client needs against team capacity?
  • What information is needed before making the call?

Workplace application

  • What steps would you take in your own workplace to make a fast decision without over-promising or damaging trust?
